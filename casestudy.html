Festa Italian Non-Profit Redesign

Role

UX/UI Designer

Team Size

Four

Duration

3 Weeks

Tools

Figma, Invision, & Miro

Context

In this school project we were tasked with a full redesign of the user expierene and user interface of a local portland non-profit website.

Problem Statement

The lack of a user friendly, secure, and official looking non-profit webpage causes potential Festa supporters to hesitate while donating and miss information on upcoming events. How might we assist users to engage with Festa Italiana so that they could feel more safe and confident in their donation or online purchasing experience?

Our Solution

Redesigning the website is meant to create an approachable and user friendly experience for anyone looking to attend and support the Festa Italiana. We also wanted to create a website that would be easily managable and editable by Festa staff.

User Persona

User Interviews

We recorded and transcribed 6 interviews targeting individuals who have either attended Festa previously or who donate to nonprofits.


Our questions targeted where people receive information about nonprofits and what makes them feel like it is a secure and safe experience.

“I have probably attended Festa seven or eight times.”


“I’ve purchased Olive Oil both online and in person. It was much easier in person, the way it’s currently ordered online is not very user friendly”


“I don’t use Facebook. I would go to their website page, through it to find information. Slow and painful way.”

-Steve Johnson

Affinity Diagram: Web Security

From our Affinity Diagram web security became a clear very important factor to festa goers. Almost all interviewees expressed that they perfer non-profits to have an offical webpage because Facebook is not as trusted. Since Festa current checkout is a link to a random paypal email this makes potential olive oil customer and donators skeptical of moving forward with their purchase.

Storyboard

UX Hypothesis

We hypothesized that by reorganizing and rebranding the website, as well as walking through each step of the transaction with the users, we will be able to maintain transparency of the information and build trust from our users.

Current Webpage

  • The current webpage was built on a free web builder and is not up to date.

  • Most of the links are broken or do not work.

  • The contrast of the text to the background does not meet accesibility guidlines.
  • The header dissapears after each click to a new tab.

  • The checkout requires a manual process of typing in your items then calculating the value on your own.

  • Volunteer signup calander non functioning

Sitemap

We then developed a new sitemap that would make it easy for users to access any information about festa, donate, volunteer, or purchase olive oil securely.

Wireframe Sketches

Mid-Fi Prototype & User Testing

We decided to make the Festa countdown our showstoppper feature, as well as an added image coursel in the hero section. We also began to add features such as apple pay to mobile to give users a more secure checkout.

Our user testing focused on making sure the basic tasks of the webpage could be done with ease. Users were asked to navigate through the checkout process and locate information about other Italian clubs, they were all succesful in doing so without asking further questions. We were also able to contact the head of Festa and ask for his feedback, he expressed that he really liked the new layout but expressed he would need help building the webpage.

Wireframe Sketches

We spoke cloesly with the current web manager at Festa. We decided on a color pallete that is accesible but also reflects the italian culture and roots. We were also given some images to work with from previous Festas.

Final Thoughts & Future Opportunities

This was an informative and engaging experience for all of us as UX/UI designers. We had the great opportunity to work closely with the stakeholders of Festa Italiana and would like to continue this project in the future.


The main constraint moving forward with development is budget and current knowledge. Festa is a non-profit with a small budget for their website, we will need to code something on our own that would be manageable by them to update and maintain.
